Quick Start Guide

To Create a Map (5 minutes):

  1. Open ALICE Master Database/counties_with_alice_2023.geojson
  2. Drag into ArcGIS Online, QGIS, or geojson.io
  3. Style by "Combined_Vulnerability_Pct" field
  4. Use color scale: Green (0-20%) ‚Üí Yellow (20-40%) ‚Üí Orange (40-60%) ‚Üí Red (60%+)
  5. Filter by Chapter, Region, or Division as needed

To Explore Interactively:

  1. Visit alice-data-explorer for choropleth maps
  2. Visit alice-redcross-atlas for Red Cross intelligence platform
  3. Visit alice-demographics for demographic analysis

To Download Data:

  1. Visit alice_master_database
  2. Download the files you need (CSV, GeoJSON, or entire repository)
  3. Read the README.md for detailed documentation

What is ALICE?

ALICE = Asset Limited, Income Constrained, Employed

ALICE households are the "working poor" - they earn above the Federal Poverty Level but below what it actually costs to live in their community. Traditional poverty measures miss 2-3x as many struggling households. ALICE data reveals the true scope of economic hardship.

Key Metrics:

  • Poverty Rate: % below Federal Poverty Level
  • ALICE Rate: % above poverty but below survival budget (working poor)
  • Combined Vulnerability: Poverty + ALICE = total households struggling
  • Above ALICE: % who can afford basic needs
